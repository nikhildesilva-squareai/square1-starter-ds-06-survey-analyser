# Data dictionary — `survey_responses.csv`

An employee/customer experience survey export. **Synthetic — generated by Square 1 AI, free for learners.** ~8,000 rows, one per submitted response.

> ⚠️ The signal you're after lives in the **free-text `comment`** and its
> relationship to `rating` — your job is to recover it with NLP, not to peek at
> how it was made. Real-world dirt is baked in: **~8% of comments are blank**
> (people skip the box), and some are **ALL-CAPS** or contain **typos**. Clean
> and handle these before you score — blank comments shouldn't count as neutral
> by accident, and casing/typos shouldn't break your tokeniser.

| Column | Type | Description |
|---|---|---|
| `respondent_id` | string | Anonymous identifier, e.g. `R100042`. Not a real person. |
| `submitted_at` | date | ISO date the response was submitted (all in 2025). |
| `department` | string | One of Engineering / Sales / Support / Marketing / Operations / People. |
| `rating` | int | Overall satisfaction, **1–5 stars**. |
| `nps` | int | Net Promoter–style score, **0–10**, derived loosely from the rating. |
| `comment` | string | Free-text open response. **Blank for ~8% of rows; some ALL-CAPS or typo'd.** |

**What to compute:** a **sentiment score** per non-blank comment (e.g. VADER compound, −1…+1); the **themes/keywords** that recur in positive vs negative comments; and a **sentiment-vs-rating check** — does comment sentiment line up with the star rating, and where does it diverge? Roll it up by `department` and into a short summary.
